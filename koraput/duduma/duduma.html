Duduma Waterfall

Nature's Majestic Symphony in Odisha

About Duduma Waterfall

Duduma Waterfall, also known as Machkund Falls, is one of the tallest and most spectacular waterfalls in Odisha. Falling from a height of 175 meters, it is located in the Koraput district amidst the lush greenery of the Eastern Ghats. The waterfall originates from the Machkund River and is a revered site for both its breathtaking natural beauty and its hydroelectric power project. It is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

Natural Beauty

Surrounded by dense forests, steep cliffs, and a pristine environment, Duduma Waterfall is a feast for the senses. The sound of gushing water, the cool mist in the air, and the verdant surroundings make it a perfect escape for those seeking tranquility and rejuvenation. The area is also home to tribal communities, adding a cultural charm to its natural allure.

Things to Do

  • Nature Walks: Explore the dense forests and picturesque surroundings.
  • Trekking: Take a trek to reach the waterfall viewpoint.
  • Photography: Capture the stunning vistas and tribal culture.
  • Picnics: Enjoy a day out with family and friends near the falls.

Plan Your Visit

Best Time to Visit

October to February for pleasant weather and lush greenery.

Entry Fee

Free entry. Parking charges may apply.

Safety Tips

Wear sturdy shoes, avoid slippery rocks, and carry water.

How to Get There

Mode Details
By Air Nearest airport is Visakhapatnam, 200 km away.
By Train Jeypore Railway Station, 70 km from Duduma.
By Road Accessible by buses and taxis from Koraput, Jeypore, or Vishakhapatnam.
