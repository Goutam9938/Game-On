Shri Ram Temple Bhubaneswar

A Sacred Journey to the Divine

About Shri Ram Temple

Shri Ram Temple, located in the heart of Bhubaneswar, is a sacred shrine dedicated to Lord Ram. Built in traditional Kalinga architecture, the temple exudes spiritual tranquility, drawing devotees and tourists alike. The temple is one of the prominent places of worship in Bhubaneswar and holds immense religious significance for the followers of Lord Ram. With its stunning carvings and intricate sculptures, the temple is a beautiful example of Odisha's architectural heritage. It stands as a beacon of devotion, offering peace and harmony to all who visit.

Famous Food & Culture

Shri Ram Temple and Bhubaneswar are deeply rooted in cultural traditions, offering a variety of local foods and experiences that reflect the region's unique culinary heritage. Some of the famous local dishes include:

  • Dalma: A lentil dish cooked with vegetables, offering a perfect blend of spices.
  • Chhena Poda: A delicious sweet made from chhena (cottage cheese), sugar, and cardamom.
  • Pakhala Bhata: A fermented rice dish served with curd, a staple in Odisha's cuisine.

Nearby Tourist Attractions

  • Udayagiri & Khandagiri Caves: Ancient rock-cut caves with historical significance, located just 8 km away from the temple.
  • Parasurameswar Temple: A beautiful Shiva temple with exceptional architecture, just 5 km from Shri Ram Temple.
  • Nandankanan Zoo: A zoological park with a variety of wildlife, located around 15 km away from the temple.

Plan Your Visit

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Shri Ram Temple is during the **winter months** (October to March) when the weather is cool and pleasant, ideal for exploring the temple and surrounding attractions.

Local Customs

Visitors should dress modestly and respectfully. Offering flowers and prayers is a common tradition when visiting the temple.

Accommodation

Bhubaneswar offers various accommodation options, including budget hotels and luxury stays, located within close proximity to Shri Ram Temple.
