Barabati Fort

Explore the History of Cuttack

About Barabati Fort

Barabati Fort, located in the heart of Cuttack, Odisha, is a historic monument dating back to the Ganga dynasty. The fort, built in the 14th century, showcases the architectural grandeur of the medieval era. The remains of its nine-storied palace and intricate carvings provide a glimpse into the past. Surrounded by lush greenery and a serene moat, Barabati Fort is a testament to the region's rich cultural heritage.

Famous Food & Culture

Cuttack is known for its street food and traditional dishes:

  • Dahibara Aloodum: A signature street food of Odisha.
  • Chhena Poda: A baked dessert made from fresh cheese and caramelized sugar.
  • Chakuli Pitha: A savory pancake enjoyed with chutneys or dalma.

Nearby Tourist Attractions

  • Netaji Birthplace Museum: A museum dedicated to Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose, located near Barabati Fort.
  • Silver City Walk: Explore Cuttack's famous silver filigree market.
  • Mahanadi River View: Enjoy a peaceful riverside view near the fort.

How to Get There

Mode Details
By Air Nearest airport is Biju Patnaik International Airport, Bhubaneswar (about 30 km).
By Train Cuttack Railway Station is around 2 km from Barabati Fort.
By Road Bus services and private taxis are available from Bhubaneswar and nearby cities.

Plan Your Visit

Best Time to Visit

Winter months (November to February) offer pleasant weather.

Local Customs

Respect the historical significance and avoid littering around the fort.

Safety Tips

Wear comfortable footwear and stay hydrated while exploring.
