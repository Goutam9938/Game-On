Akhanda Temple

Explore the Serenity of Cuttack

About Akhanda Temple, Cuttack

Akhanda Temple is one of the historic landmarks in Cuttack, Odisha. It represents a unique blend of traditional temple architecture and serene surroundings. The temple is renowned for its intricate carvings and its association with local legends. Devotees visit Akhanda Temple not only for spiritual solace but also to witness its historic significance in the cultural fabric of Odisha.

Famous Food & Culture

Cuttack is famous for its traditional delicacies and cultural vibrancy:

  • Dahibara Aloodum: A tangy and spicy street food unique to Cuttack.
  • Chhena Poda: A caramelized sweet dish made with cottage cheese.
  • Biryani: Known for its flavor-rich Odia style.

Nearby Tourist Attractions

  • Barabati Fort: A historic site symbolizing Odisha's rich cultural past.
  • Chandi Mandir: A temple dedicated to Goddess Chandi, located near Barabati Fort.
  • Odisha Maritime Museum: A fascinating museum showcasing Odisha's maritime heritage.

How to Get There

Mode Details
By Air Biju Patnaik International Airport, Bhubaneswar, is the nearest airport (about 35 km away).
By Train Cuttack Railway Station is the nearest station, just 5 km from the temple.
By Road Well-connected by buses and taxis; Cuttack is accessible from all major towns in Odisha.

Plan Your Visit

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Akhanda Temple is during the winter months for pleasant weather.

Local Customs and Culture

Respect local customs and participate in cultural festivals to enjoy the true essence of Cuttack.

Safety Tips

Carry water, avoid crowded areas during festivals, and dress appropriately for temple visits.
