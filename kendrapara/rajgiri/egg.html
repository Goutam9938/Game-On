Rajgiri Waterfall

Discover the Hidden Beauty of Nayagarh

About Rajgiri Waterfall & Hill Stations

Rajgiri Waterfall is a stunning waterfall located in Nayagarh district, Odisha, surrounded by lush greenery and picturesque landscapes. The water cascades from the hilltop, creating a breathtaking view and offering a peaceful escape from urban life.

The nearby Rajgiri Hill Station provides a great spot for trekking, nature photography, and sightseeing. With its serene environment, it attracts tourists, nature lovers, and adventurers alike.

Famous Food & Culture

Nayagarh is known for its traditional Odiya cuisine. Some of the popular dishes include:

  • Dalma: A delicious lentil and vegetable stew.
  • Chhenapoda: A baked cheese dessert, known as the ‘burnt cheese cake’ of Odisha.
  • Khicede: A rice and lentil dish often served in temples.

Nearby Tourist Attractions

  • Lingaraj Temple: A historic temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located 85 km from Nayagarh.
  • Kuanria Dam: A scenic spot surrounded by forests, ideal for picnics and boating, located on the Mahanadi River.
  • Nayagarh Fort: An ancient fort with historical significance and beautiful architectural details.

How to Get There

Mode Details
By Air The nearest airport is Biju Patnaik International Airport, Bhubaneswar (about 120 km away).
By Train The closest railway station is Khurda Road Junction, 90 km away from Nayagarh.
By Road Rajgiri is well connected by road. You can hire a taxi or take a bus from Bhubaneswar or Cuttack.

Plan Your Visit

Best Time to Visit

October to February is the ideal time to visit Rajgiri Waterfall and Hill Station. The weather during these months is pleasant, making it perfect for sightseeing, trekking, and enjoying nature.

Local Customs and Culture

Experience the vibrant culture of Odisha by participating in local festivals like Rath Yatra and Durga Puja. Respect the traditional dress codes and customs of the local community to make your visit enjoyable and immersive.

Safety Tips for Visitors

Wear sturdy footwear for trekking. Avoid visiting during the monsoon due to slippery trails. Always carry a first-aid kit and stay hydrated. Respect nature by avoiding littering and sticking to marked paths.
