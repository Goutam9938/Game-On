Kantilo Nilamadhaba Temple

Discover the Hidden Beauty of Nayagarh

About Kantilo Nilamadhaba Temple

The Kantilo Nilamadhaba Temple is an ancient and prominent Hindu temple located in the Kendrapara district of Odisha, India. It is dedicated to Lord Nilamadhaba, a form of Lord Vishnu. The temple is set in a peaceful and serene environment, nestled along the banks of the Luna River.

Historical Significance:

The temple holds great religious and historical importance in the region, attracting devotees from all parts of India, particularly during the Rath Yatra (chariot festival) and other major Hindu festivals.

Famous Food & Culture

Nayagarh is known for its traditional Odiya cuisine. Some of the popular dishes include:

  • Kanika: Kanika is a sweet rice dish often served during religious festivals. The rice is cooked with sugar, ghee, cardamom, and raisins, creating a fragrant and sweet delicacy. It’s often enjoyed as a dessert or offering in temples.
  • Kakara Pitha: Kakara Pitha is a fried sweet made from rice flour and filled with a mixture of jaggery and coconut. After frying, it is coated with sugar syrup. It's a popular snack or dessert in the region, especially during special occasions and festivals.
  • Chhena Jhili: Chhena Jhili is a deep-fried sweet made from chhena, similar to Rasgulla, but it is fried before being soaked in sugar syrup. The golden, crispy exterior and the soft, syrup-soaked interior make it a beloved sweet in the region

Nearby Tourist Attractions

  • Darpanarayanpur Dam (Approx. 25 km from Kantilo):This serene dam is a popular picnic spot surrounded by natural beauty. It’s ideal for visitors looking to relax and enjoy the scenic landscape with a peaceful ambiance.
  • Godimada Forest (Approx. 20 km from Kantilo):A lush, green forest area near Kantilo, perfect for nature walks and birdwatching. The forest offers a tranquil escape into nature’s beauty, ideal for eco-tourism enthusiasts
  • Katradhari Dam (Approx. 35 km from Kantilo)This dam is a peaceful spot for nature lovers, offering opportunities for picnics and leisurely outings amidst beautiful surroundings.

How to Get There

Mode Details
By Air The nearest airport is Biju Patnaik International Airport, Bhubaneswar.
By Train Nearest Railway Station : Nayagarh Railway Station (3 km) and Khordha Road Railway Station (69 km)
By Road Tourists can reach Kantilo by choosing the state highway that is connected from Bhubaneswar to Kalapathara. It is located approximately 100 kms away from state capital Bhubaneswar and 33 kms from district head quarter Nayagarh.

Local Customs and Culture

Kantilo, known for the Nilamadhaba Temple, is a spiritual hub reflecting Odisha's rich heritage. Pilgrims take holy dips in the Mahanadi, offer prayers, and enjoy sacred Mahaprasad. Festivals like Rath Yatra and Makar Sankranti bring vibrant celebrations. Local art, cuisine, and traditions create a harmonious blend of spirituality, culture, and devotion.

Safety Tips for Visitors

Visitors to Kantilo should stay cautious, dress modestly, follow temple etiquette, and avoid deep areas in the Mahanadi River. Stay hydrated, secure belongings, and be alert in forests. Plan visits during the day and respect local customs for safety.
