Deomali Hilltop

The Highest Peak of Odisha

About Deomali Hilltop

Deomali Hilltop, located in the Koraput district of Odisha, is the highest peak in the state, standing at an altitude of 1,672 meters. Known for its stunning views of the surrounding forests and valleys, Deomali offers a serene escape for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. The hill is covered with lush green vegetation and is a popular spot for trekking, camping, and bird watching. It is an ideal destination for those seeking solitude and tranquility in the midst of nature.

Famous Food & Culture

Koraput and the surrounding areas are known for their unique tribal culture and delicious local food. Some of the popular dishes you can try during your visit include:

  • Mandia Roti: A traditional millet-based bread, often served with curry or chutney.
  • Chudka Pitha: A local sweet made from rice flour, jaggery, and coconut, often consumed during festivals.
  • Ragi Mudhi: A type of puffed ragi, often served with curd or curry for breakfast.

Nearby Tourist Attractions

  • Jagannath Temple, Koraput: A beautiful temple dedicated to Lord Jagannath, located around 50 km from Deomali Hilltop.
  • Waterfalls of Koraput: The district is home to several stunning waterfalls, such as the Kolab Waterfalls and Duduma Waterfalls.
  • Tribal Museum, Koraput: A museum showcasing the rich culture and heritage of the local tribes, located around 45 km from Deomali Hilltop.

Plan Your Visit

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Deomali Hilltop is during the **winter months** (October to March) when the weather is cool and pleasant, perfect for trekking and sightseeing.

Local Customs

It’s important to respect the local tribal cultures when visiting. Visitors should ask for permission before taking photographs of the locals and their customs.

Accommodation

Several hotels and guesthouses are available in and around Koraput, providing a comfortable stay. Popular options include the **Hotel Saptagiri** and **Tribal Resort, Koraput**, located within a short drive from Deomali Hilltop.
